---
title: Why a 3D blog?
date: 2026-05-05
slug: why-a-3d-blog
---

I could not, for the life of me, bring myself to make a normal website. You know the kind — a clean sans-serif name in the top left, three navigation links, a grid of project cards with rounded corners and hover effects, perhaps a dark mode toggle for personality. I've seen about four hundred of those in my life and I'm quite sure nobody has ever looked at one and felt anything other than mild professional approval.

I wanted something that felt like walking into a room. Not a portfolio, not a resume, not an "about me" with a headshot where I'm smiling at a forty-five degree angle. A room, with things in it — a desk, a notebook, a mug, the kind of clutter that tells you more about a person than any "passionate developer with a love for clean code" bio ever could.

Whether this is a profound statement about how context shapes meaning or an elaborate procrastination mechanism dressed up as philosophy, I cannot tell you. I suspect it's both. A blog post read off a notebook on a sunlit desk arrives differently than the same words on a white page with a sans-serif header — or at least, that's what I'm telling myself to justify the ungodly number of hours I've spent adjusting the position of a virtual armchair.

I suppose we'll find out if the idea holds any water, or if I've simply built myself a very pretty room to be lonely in.
